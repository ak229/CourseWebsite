Due Date

Friday, March 11 @ 2 hours after 11:59pm (2am Saturday morning)

Objectives

  • Create a website to pitch a project idea for this course
  • Host the site publicly on the Internet
  • Practice with HTML, CSS, and Bootstrap
  • Use FTP (File Transfer Protocol) to upload files to a server

Resources

  • W3Schools HTML: http://www.w3schools.com/html/default.asp
  • W3Schools CSS: http://www.w3schools.com/css/default.asp
  • W3Schools BootStrap: http://www.w3schools.com/bootstrap/default.asp
  • W3Schools Templates: http://www.w3schools.com/bootstrap/bootstrap_templates.asp

Your objective is to create a web page to pitch an idea for your final project in this course that will be available to all your classmates, and anyone with an Internet connection. For the final project you will work in groups of 1-3 of your choosing and you can use these pitches to help form groups with others who have similar interests and ideas.

Final project note: You will be required to apply each of the topics covered in the course in your project to make a full-stack website that includes a CGI call to a python script that connects to a database. The topic for your final project will be open-ended so you can choose anything you'd like for the application of this technology.

You can view your classmates pitches on the course website: Project Pitches

Project Pitch with Bootstrap


Use a Bootstrap template and populate it with your project pitch. Several templates can be found on W3Schools, though you are not limited to only these templates. If you do not want to use a template, you can choose to create your site from scratch. If you create your site from scratch, it must use Bootstrap elements as well as CSS.

Your project pitch must be descriptive as to what you want to accomplish in the final project for this course.



Contact Information
